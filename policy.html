Who We Are

Gratis Pages (gratispages.netlify.app) is a free digital library that provides access to PDF books at no cost and without requiring user registration. The site is owned and operated by Mahadi Islam Alif, an individual based in Bangladesh.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how it is used, and what rights you have in relation to it. By using this website, you agree to the practices described here.

In plain language: We do not ask you to create an account. We do not sell your data. We use Google's standard services (analytics, advertising) that use cookies — and this policy tells you exactly what those do and how to opt out.
02

Information We Collect

A. Information You Provide Directly

We only receive personal data you actively submit. Currently, the only form on Gratis Pages is the Book Request Form, which collects:

  • Book title — the book you are requesting
  • Author name — optional, to help us locate the book
  • Email address — so we can notify you when your request is fulfilled
  • Optional note — any additional context you choose to share

This form is processed through Formspree (see Section 9). We never use it for marketing without your explicit consent.

B. Information Collected Automatically

When you visit Gratis Pages, certain technical information is automatically collected by our third-party service providers (Google Analytics, Google AdSense). This may include:

  • IP address — used to determine approximate geographic region (country/city level), not stored by us directly
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system and device type
  • Pages visited, time on page, and navigation paths
  • Referral source — how you arrived at the site (e.g. Google Search, direct link)
  • Session duration and bounce rate
  • Cookie identifiers — assigned by Google services for analytics and ad personalisation

We do not collect: passwords, payment information, government ID numbers, or any sensitive personal data. We do not build individual user profiles.

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Gratis Pages uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to make our services work properly and to serve personalised advertisements. When you first visit, a cookie consent banner is displayed.

Strict Consent Enforcement: Advertising and analytical tracking cookies (such as Google Analytics and Google AdSense) are completely blocked by our code from loading on your device until you explicitly click "Accept Cookies" on our banner.

Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie / Identifier Provider Purpose Duration
_ga, _ga_* Google Analytics 4 Distinguish unique visitors, track sessions and page views 2 years
_gid Google Analytics 4 Distinguish users within a 24-hour session 24 hours
IDE, DSID Google DoubleClick (AdSense/AdMob) Personalise and measure ad effectiveness 13 months
test_cookie Google DoubleClick Check whether browser supports cookies Session
NID, CONSENT Google Preference storage and consent tracking 6 months – 2 years
gp_cookie_consent Gratis Pages (localStorage) Remember your cookie consent choice Persistent (localStorage)

Managing & Opting Out of Cookies

You have full control over cookies. You may:

  • Click "Decline" on the cookie banner — non-essential cookies will never be set
  • Clear the gp_cookie_consent key from your browser's localStorage at any time to reset your choice
  • Use your browser settings to block or delete cookies globally
  • Opt out of Google Analytics tracking via Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-On
  • Opt out of personalised Google ads via Google Ads Settings or aboutads.info
  • Visit Your Online Choices (EU) for interest-based advertising preferences

Google AdSense, AdMob & Advertising

Gratis Pages uses Google AdSense and Google AdMob to display advertisements. These services are provided by Google LLC (or Google Ireland Limited for users in the EEA/UK). Advertising revenue helps us keep the library free for everyone.

Google may use cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies to show you advertisements based on your past visits to this and other websites. This is known as interest-based advertising or remarketing.

Google's EU User Consent Policy: For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), Google requires that we obtain your consent before enabling personalised advertising. Our cookie consent banner fulfils this requirement. If you decline, only non-personalised (contextual) ads may be shown, or no ads at all.

Google AdSense uses the DoubleClick cookie (IDE) to serve ads and report on ad performance. You can opt out of personalised ads from Google by visiting google.com/settings/ads.

For Google AdMob (mobile advertising), Google collects device identifiers such as the Android Advertising ID or Apple IDFA, subject to your device-level privacy settings. You can reset or opt out of interest-based ads via your device settings: Settings → Privacy → Ads (iOS) or Settings → Google → Ads (Android).

We do not pass any personally identifiable information to Google's advertising platforms. All data processed by Google is governed by the Google Privacy Policy.

07

Google Analytics 4

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors interact with Gratis Pages. GA4 collects data such as page views, session durations, device types, and general geographic location (country/city — never street address).

IP Anonymisation: GA4 anonymises IP addresses by default. We do not receive or store full IP addresses through Google Analytics.

Data Retention: We have configured GA4 to retain user-level and event-level data for 14 months, after which it is automatically deleted from Google's servers.

Data Sharing: We have disabled all data-sharing settings with Google products and services beyond what is necessary to operate Analytics. We do not use GA4 to share data for advertising purposes independently.

You may opt out of GA4 tracking at any time by installing the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-On. GA4 data is processed under a Data Processing Agreement between us and Google, in compliance with GDPR Article 28.

Google Search Console

Google Search Console is a webmaster tool provided by Google that allows us to monitor and improve Gratis Pages' visibility in Google Search results. It does not collect personally identifiable information about individual visitors.

Search Console provides aggregated data only: which search queries lead users to our site, click-through rates, impressions, and technical health metrics (crawl errors, page speed, mobile usability). No individual user is identifiable from this data.

Search Console data is accessible only to us as the verified site owner and is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. It is used solely for the purpose of improving the website's search engine presence and technical performance.

Formspree (Book Request Form)

Our Book Request Form is powered by Formspree, a third-party form submission service. When you submit a book request, the following data is sent to and stored by Formspree:

Formspree stores form submissions on secure servers in the United States. They are GDPR compliant and process data under a Data Processing Agreement. Their full privacy policy is available at formspree.io/legal/privacy-policy.

We use the data you submit only to respond to your book request. We do not use your email for marketing communications, and we do not share it with any other third party.

To request deletion of a form submission, email us at iamitachi494@gmail.com with the subject line "Delete My Form Data."

Your Rights — GDPR (EU / EEA Users)

If you are located in the European Union or European Economic Area, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) gives you the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of Access (Art. 15) — You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification (Art. 16) — You may request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected.
  • Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Art. 17) — You may request deletion of your personal data where there is no compelling reason for us to continue processing it.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18) — You may request that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability (Art. 20) — You may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format for transfer to another controller.
  • Right to Object (Art. 21) — You may object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right not to be subject to automated decision-making (Art. 22) — We do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal effects.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — Where processing is based on consent (e.g. advertising cookies), you may withdraw it at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
How to exercise your rights: Email us at iamitachi494@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. If you believe we have violated your rights, you may lodge a complaint with your national Data Protection Authority (DPA). A list of EU DPAs is available at edpb.europa.eu.

Our lawful bases for processing under GDPR are: consent (advertising cookies), legitimate interests (analytics, security), and contract performance (book request notifications).

Your Rights — CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), provides you with specific rights regarding your personal information.

  • Right to Know — You may request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, as well as our business or commercial purpose for collecting it.
  • Right to Delete — You may request deletion of personal information we have collected, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Correct — You may request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing — We do not sell or share personal information as defined by the CCPA. We do not sell data to data brokers or third-party advertisers for their own use.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information — We do not collect sensitive personal information as defined by the CPRA.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination — We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.

To submit a CCPA request, email us at iamitachi494@gmail.com with the subject line "CCPA Privacy Request." We will respond within 45 days.

Note: Because we do not sell personal information, certain CCPA provisions regarding opt-out of sale may not apply to our operations. However, we honour all rights listed above in good faith.
